A note I should say more often

Some things are hard to say out loud. So I built you a whole world to say them in.

You never asked for thanks. You just showed up — every single day, in every small way that I'm only now old enough to notice.

Half of who I am is just me trying to be a little more like you. The patience. The quiet stubbornness. The way you make hard things look simple.

So this is me, slowing down for a second, to say it properly.

Through the years

A few moments, out of a lifetime of them.

Day one

You carried me before I could walk.

And somehow you've never really put me down since.

Growing up

Bikes, exams, late nights.

You were patient with all of it — even the parts I'd want a do-over on.

The lessons

You taught me to keep my word.

Not with a speech. Just by being a man who always kept his.

Now

I'm building things I hope make you proud.

You're still the first person I want to tell good news to.

Things I don't say enough

04You're my first phone call, good day or bad.
05Everything good in me started with you.

Thank you for everything, Papa.

No website is ever going to be enough. But I hope it made you smile.
